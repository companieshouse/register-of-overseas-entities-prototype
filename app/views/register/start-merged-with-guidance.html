What the Register of Overseas Entities is

The Register of Overseas Entities has come into force in the UK through the new Economic Crime (Transparency and Enforcement) Act 2022.

Overseas entities must register with Companies House and tell us who their registrable beneficial owners or managing officers are, so that they can buy, sell or transfer property or land in the UK. Overseas entities who already own land in the UK will have until XX/XX/XX to register their beneficial owners or managing officers.

After registering, the overseas entity will get a unique Overseas Entity ID number to give to the Land Registry when it buys, sells or transfers UK property or land.

This will lead to more transparency, which will allow law enforcement agencies to investigate suspicious wealth more effectively.

What an overseas entity is

This is a legal entity, such as a company or organisation, that is governed by the law of a country or territory outside the UK.

Read the guidance about overseas entities

What a beneficial owner is

A beneficial owner is any legal body that has significant influence or control over the overseas entity. It can be:

  • an individual person
  • an other legal entity
  • a government or public authority
  • a trustee of a trust
  • a member of a firm that is not a legal person under its governing law

You’ll need to give us details for any registrable beneficial owners that meet one or more of the conditions known as the natures of control.

Who can use this service

You can use this service if you work at, or work on behalf of, the overseas entity.

It’s quicker and easier for an overseas entity to be registered by the same UK-regulated agent that carried out its identity checks. These can include financial institutions and legal professionals.

What information you need to submit

You'll need to provide information about the overseas entity and any registrable beneficial owners.

Overseas entity information

You'll need to tell us its:

  • name
  • the country it was formed in
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • an email address at the overseas entity we can send important information to, including the Overseas Entity ID
  • its legal form and governing law
  • the public register it’s on and its registration number (if relevant)

You'll also need to tell us if the entity has disposed of UK property or land between 28 February 2022 and [date of launch].

Beneficial owner information: individual person

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • nationality
  • correspondence address and home address
  • the date they became a beneficial owner for the overseas entity
  • nature of control

You'll also need to tell us:

  • if they’re on the UK Sanctions List
  • if any of the entity’s beneficial owners have ever applied to protect personal information at Companies House

Beneficial owner information: other legal entity

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • the public register it’s on and its registration number (if relevant)
  • the date they became a beneficial owner for the overseas entity
  • nature of control

You'll need to tell us if they’re on the UK Sanctions List.

Beneficial owner information: government or public authority

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • the date they became a beneficial owner for the overseas entity
  • nature of control

If no beneficial owners can be identified

If there are no beneficial owners, you'll need to give us information about the overseas entity's managing officers. This is a director, manager or company secretary of the overseas entity.

Managing officer information: individual

You'll need to tell us each managing officer's:

  • full name (and former names, if relevant)
  • date of birth
  • nationality
  • correspondence address and home address
  • occupation (this is optional)
  • roles and responsibilities in relation to the entity

Managing officer information: corporate

You'll need to tell us each managing officer's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • the public register it’s on and its registration number (if relevant)

How to submit information about trusts

If any trustees of a trust are registrable beneficial owners, you’ll need to give us information about that trust. This includes details about people or entities that are:

  • current or past beneficial owners
  • beneficiaries
  • settlors
  • grantors
  • interested persons

To submit trust information, download the Trust Excel document (52KB).

You'll need to:

  • open the document in Excel
  • select the button to enable macros when prompted

Then follow the instructions within the document to enter the information into the service. You'll need to complete a new document for each trust.

It’s important to complete the Excel document before you start using this service, as the service will time out if you do not use it for 60 minutes.

What is needed for identity checks

A UK-regulated agent must complete identity checks on all beneficial owners and managing officers of an overseas entity before it can be registered.

They must be based in the UK and supervised under the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds Regulations 2017. They can be an individual or a corporate entity, such as a financial institution or legal professional.

Agent assurance code

The UK-regulated agent will need to request an agent assurance code from Companies House. This confirms that the agent has carried out identity checks. We cannot register an overseas entity without a code.

What information will be shown on the public register

Most of the information given to Companies House about overseas entities, beneficial owners and managing officers will be publicly available on the Register of Overseas Entities.

We will not show:

  • home addresses
  • email addresses
  • full dates of birth – only the month and year will be shown
  • information about trusts, however it may be shared with HMRC

Apply to register an overseas entity and its beneficial owners

You'll need:

  • to sign in to or create a Companies House account
  • to give us the name and email address of someone we can contact about the application
  • to give information about the overseas entity and its beneficial owners or managing officers
  • to confirm that identity checks have been completed by a third-party agent based in the UK
  • a credit or debit card to pay the £X fee - for now, you cannot pay with a Companies House payment account

What happens after an overseas entity is registered

If the application is accepted, the overseas entity and its beneficial owners and managing officers will be added to the Register of Overseas Entities.

If it's rejected, we'll email you to let you know what to do next and refund the £X fee.

The Overseas Entity ID must be given to the Land Registry whenever it buys, sells or transfers land or property in the UK. We’ll send this to the overseas entity’s email address.

Updating information on the register

The overseas entity must file a statement at least once a year to confirm that the information on the Register of Overseas Entities is up to date. It can file earlier if it needs to update any information.

Removing an overseas entity

An overseas entity can only be removed from the register if it no longer owns any property or land in the UK. Apply to remove an overseas entity from the register by emailing roe@companieshouse.gov.uk.
