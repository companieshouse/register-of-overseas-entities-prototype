What the Register of Overseas Entities is

The Register of Overseas Entities has come into force in the UK through the new Economic Crime (Transparency and Enforcement) Act 2022. Overseas entities must register with Companies House and tell us who their beneficial owners or managing officers are, so that they can buy, sell or transfer property or land in the UK.

After registering, the details of the overseas entity and its beneficial owners will be publicly available on the online register.

This will lead to more transparency, which will allow law enforcement agencies to investigate suspicious wealth more effectively.

What an overseas entity is

This is a legal entity, such as a company or organisation, that is governed by the law of a country or territory outside the UK.

What a beneficial owner is

A beneficial owner is any legal body that has significant influence or control over the overseas entity. It can be an individual person, other legal entity, government or public authority.

You will need to provide details for any beneficial owners that meet one or more of the following conditions known as the 'nature of control':

The beneficial owner is an individual person, other legal entity, government or public authority and:

  • holds, directly or indirectly, more than 25% of the shares in the entity
  • holds, directly or indirectly, more than 25% of the voting rights in the entity
  • holds the right, directly or indirectly, to appoint or remove a majority of the board of directors of the entity
  • has the right to exercise, or actually exercises, significant influence or control over the entity

The beneficial owner is a trustee of a trust and:

  • the trustees of that trust (in their capacity as such) hold, directly or indirectly, more than 25% of the shares in the entity
  • the trustees of that trust (in their capacity as such) hold, directly or indirectly, more than 25% of the voting rights in the entity
  • the trustees of that trust (in their capacity as such) hold the right, directly or indirectly, to appoint or remove a majority of the board of directors of the company
  • the trustees of that trust (in their capacity as such) have the right to exercise, or actually exercise, significant influence or control over the company

The beneficial owner is a member of a firm that is not a legal person under its governing law, and:

  • the members of that firm (in their capacity as such) hold, directly or indirectly, more than 25% of the shares in the entity
  • the members of that firm (in their capacity as such) hold, directly or indirectly, more than 25% of the voting rights in the entity
  • the members of that firm (in their capacity as such) hold the right, directly or indirectly, to appoint or remove a majority of the board of directors of the company
  • the members of that firm (in their capacity as such) have the right to exercise, or actually exercise, significant influence or control over the company

Who can use this service

You can use this service if you’re:

  • someone who represents the overseas entity
  • a third-party agent based in the UK

When you need to register

Overseas entities need to be on the Register of Overseas Entities before they can buy, sell or transfer property or land in the UK. Once registered, the overseas entity will be provided with a unique Overseas Entity ID number, which must be given to the Land Registry on completion of the purchase, sale or transfer of property or land.

What information you need to submit

You'll need to provide information about the overseas entity and any beneficial owners.

Overseas entity information

You'll need to tell us its:

  • name
  • contact details
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • country of registration and registration number

Beneficial owner information: individual person

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • correspondence address and home address
  • nationality
  • date of appointment
  • nature of control

Beneficial owner information: other legal entity, government or public authority

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • country of registration and registration number
  • date of appointment
  • nature of control

If no beneficial owners can be identified

If there are no beneficial owners, you'll need to provide information about the overseas entity's managing officers.

{{ govukDetails({ summaryText: "What is a managing officer?", text: "This is a director, manager or company secretary of the overseas entity." }) }}

Managing officer: individual

You'll need to tell us each managing officer's:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • correspondence address and home address
  • nationality
  • business occupation
  • roles and responsibilities

Managing officer: corporate

You'll need to tell us each managing officer's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • country of registration and registration number

Trusts

If any of the beneficial owners are trustees of a trust in terms of their nature of control, you will also need to provide information about that trust. This includes details about the beneficiaries, historical beneficial owners, settlors, grantors and interested persons.

You’ll need to complete a Trust information document (Excel 462KB). We highly recommend that you complete this before you start using the service. Then follow the instructions within the document to enter the information into the service.

You'll need to complete a new document for each trust.

What identity checks are needed

You must confirm that identity checks have been carried out on all beneficial owners and managing officers. These identity checks must be done by a third-party agent who is based in the UK, such as an accountant or solicitor.

You'll need to tell us the agent's:

  • name
  • correspondence address
  • email address
  • supervisory body
  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration number, if applicable

You'll also need:

  • the agent code, which is provided by Companies House
  • the name of the person responsible for overseeing identity checks

What information will be shown on the public register

Most of the information that is submitted about overseas entities, beneficial owners and managing officers will be publicly available on the Register of Overseas Entities.

We will not show:

  • home addresses
  • email addresses
  • full dates of birth – only the month and year will be shown
  • information about trusts (although it may be shared with HMRC)

Apply to register an overseas entity and its beneficial owners

You'll need:

  • to sign in to or create a Companies House account
  • to give us the name and email address of someone we can contact about the application
  • a credit or debit card to pay the £X fee

What happens after an overseas entity is registered

It can take up to X working days to process an application to register an overseas entity and its beneficial owners. If the application is accepted, the overseas entity and its beneficial owners and managing officers will be added to the Register of Overseas Entities.

If it's rejected, we'll email you to let you know what to do next.

We will also send the Overseas Entity ID number by email. This ID number must be provided to the Land Registry whenever the overseas entity buys, sells or transfers land or property in the UK.

Updating information on the register

The overseas entity must file a statement at least once a year to confirm that the information on the Register of Overseas Entities is up to date.

It may choose to file sooner if it needs to update the information before the next statement is due to be filed.

Removing an overseas entity

An overseas entity can only be removed from the register if it no longer owns any property or land in the UK. Apply to remove an overseas entity from the register by emailing roe@companieshouse.gov.uk.
