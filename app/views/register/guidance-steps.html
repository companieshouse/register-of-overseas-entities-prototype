Guidance

Step by step: How to register an overseas entity

There are different steps to registering and this depends on who you are and who is registering for you. The process is also different if you are registering online or via paper form.

You must register via paper form only if:
  • any beneficial owners have their personal information protected at Companies House (or are waiting for the results of an application)
  • the overseas entity has disposed of UK property or land since 28 February 2022

You are a UK-regulated agent and you are registering for the overseas entity

You are doing the verification checks and registering the overseas entity using our online service.

  1. Read the guidance and gather all information
    If relevant, also download and complete the Trust Excel document. (52KB)
  2. Check if someone from your organisation has already requested an agent assurance code.
    If they have then go to step 6 in this list
  3. Download the 'Request an agent assurance code' form. (ODT, 9KB)
    You get one code per organisation.

  4. Complete the agent assurance code request form and email it to agentcode@companieshouse.gov.uk
  5. Companies House sends your organisation’s agent assurance code via email (do not share outside of your organisation)
  6. Complete the verification checks (see our guidance TBC)
  7. Register the overseas entity and confirm the verification checks information
  8. On completion, Companies House emails you an automatic response email with your application number
  9. Companies House emails you to confirm if the registration is successful

You are UK-regulated agent and you are registering for an overseas entity on paper

  1. Read the guidance and gather all information
    If relevant, also download and complete the Trust Excel document. (52KB)
  2. Check if someone from your organisation has already requested an agent assurance code.
    If they have then go to step 6 in this list
  3. Download the 'Request an agent assurance code' form. (ODT, 9KB)
    You get one code per organisation.

  4. Complete the agent assurance code request form and email it to agentcode@companieshouse.gov.uk
  5. Companies House emails you your organisation’s agent assurance code (do not share outside of your organisation)
  6. Complete the verification checks (see our guidance TBC)
  7. Contact Companies House to request a paper form
  8. Fill out the form to register the overseas entity and confirm verification checks
  9. Send in your completed form via post/email to Companies House
  10. You will receive an automatic email with your application number
  11. Companies House sends the overseas entity number via email and confirms that the registration is successful

You are a UK-regulated agent and the overseas entity is registering

You are doing the verification checks only.

  1. Read the guidance and gather all information
    If relevant, also download and complete the Trust Excel document. (52KB)
  2. Check if someone from your organisation has already requested an agent assurance code.
    If they have then go to step 6 in this list
  3. Download the 'Request an agent assurance code' form. (ODT, 9KB)
    You get one code per organisation.

  4. Complete the agent assurance code request form and email it to agentcode@companieshouse.gov.uk
  5. Companies House sends your organisation’s agent assurance code via email (do not share outside of your organisation)
  6. The overseas entity registers (this can happen whilst you complete 1-5)
  7. The overseas entity contacts you with their OE application number
  8. Download the 'Overseas entity verification checks statement' document (ODT, 13KB)
  9. Complete the Overseas entity verification checks statement including their OE application number and email it to agentcode@companieshouse.gov.uk
  10. Companies House sends the overseas entity number via email and confirms that the registration is successful

You are the overseas entity and a UK-regulated agent is registering for you

You will be asking an agent to do your verification checks and register for you.

  1. Read the guidance and gather all information
  2. Contact a UK-regulated agent to let them know you want them to do your verification checks and your registration
  3. Provide all information required for the registration to the agent (see our guidance)
  4. Companies House sends the overseas entity number via email and confirms that the registration is successful

You are an overseas entity and you, or someone on behalf of you is registering

You or someone on behalf of you will be registering using our online service and asking a UK-regulated agent to complete the verification checks

  1. Read the guidance and gather all information
    If relevant, also download and complete the Trust Excel document. (52KB)
  2. Contact a UK-regulated agent to let them know you want them to do your verification checks only
  3. Register the overseas entity (this is done before the agent can complete your verification checks)
  4. On completion, you will receive an email with your application number
  5. Give this application number and all information required for verification checks to your UK-regulated agent
  6. Companies House sends the overseas entity number via email and confirms that the registration is successful

You are an overseas entity and you, or someone on behalf of you is registering using our paper form

You or someone on behalf of you (not a third-part agent) will be registering using our paper form and asking a UK-regulated agent to complete the verification checks

  1. Read the guidance and gather all information
    If relevant, also download and complete the Trust Excel document. (52KB)
  2. Contact a UK-regulated agent to let them know you want them to do your verification checks only
  3. Email Companies House to request a paper form
  4. Once received, register the overseas entity using the form
  5. Send in your completed form via post/email to Companies House
  6. You will receive an automatic email with an application number
  7. Give this application number and all information required for verification checks to your UK-regulated agent
  8. Companies House sends the overseas entity number via email and confirms that the registration is successful
