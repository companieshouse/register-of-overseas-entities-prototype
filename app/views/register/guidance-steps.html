Guidance

How to register an overseas entity

There are different steps to registering and this depends on who you are and who is registering for you. You may also be registering via our paper forms.

You are the agent and you are registering for an overseas entity online

You are doing the identity checks and registering the overseas entity via our online service

  1. Read the guidance and gather all data required
  2. Check if someone in your organisation has already requested an assurance code, if yes then skip to 6 in this list
  3. Download the assurance code request form (one code per organisation)
  4. Email Companies House the completed assurance code request form
  5. Companies House emails you your organisation’s assurance code (do not share outside of your organisation)
  6. Complete the identity checks (see our guidance)
  7. Register the overseas entity and confirm identity checks information
  8. On completion, you will receive an automatic response email with your application number
  9. Companies House emails you to confirm if the registration is successful

You are the agent and you are registering for an overseas entity on paper

You are doing the identity checks and registering the overseas entity via our paper forms

  1. Read the guidance and gather all data required
  2. Check if someone in your organisation has already requested an assurance code, if yes then skip to 6 in this list
  3. Download the assurance code request form (one code per organisation)
  4. Email Companies House the completed assurance code request form
  5. Companies House emails you your organisation’s assurance code (do not share outside of your organisation)
  6. Complete the identity checks (see our guidance)
  7. Register the overseas entity and confirm identity checks information
  8. Send in your application via post/email to Companies House
  9. You will receive an email with your application number
  10. Companies House emails you to confirm if the registration is successful

You are an agent and the overseas entity is registering

You are doing the identity checks only

  1. Read the guidance and gather all data required
  2. Check if someone in your organisation has already requested an assurance code, if yes then skip to 6 in this list
  3. Download the assurance code request form (one code per organisation)
  4. Email Companies House the completed assurance code request form
  5. Companies House emails you your organisation’s assurance code (do not share outside of your organisation)
  6. The overseas entity registers(this can happen whilst you complete 1-5)
  7. The overseas entity contacts you with their OE application number
  8. Download the identity checks form
  9. Complete the identity checks and add their OE application number to the form
  10. Email Companies House the completed identity checks form
  11. Companies House emails the overseas entity to confirm if the registration is successful

You are the overseas entity and an agent is registering for you

You will be asking a third-party agent to do your identity checks and register for you.

  1. Read the guidance and gather all data required
  2. Contact a third-party agent to let them know you want them to do your identity checks and your registration
  3. Provide all information required for the registration to the third party (see our guidance)
  4. Companies House emails you to confirm if the registration is successful

You are an overseas entity and you or someone on behalf of you (not a third-party agent) is registering for you

You will be registering for yourself

  1. Read the guidance and gather all data required
  2. Contact a third-party agent to let them know you want them to do your identity checks and your registration
  3. Provide all information required for the registration to the third party (see our guidance)
  4. Companies House emails you to confirm if the registration is successful
