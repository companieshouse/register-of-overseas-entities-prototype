What the Register of Overseas Entities is

The Register of Overseas Entities has come into force in the UK through the new Economic Crime (Transparency and Enforcement) Act 2022.

Overseas entities must register with Companies House and tell us who their registrable beneficial owners or managing officers are, so that they can buy, sell or transfer property or land in the UK. Overseas entities who already own land in the UK will have until XX/XX/XX to register their beneficial owners or managing officers.

After registering, the overseas entity will get a unique Overseas Entity ID number to give to the Land Registry when it buys, sells or transfers UK property or land.

This will lead to more transparency, which will allow law enforcement agencies to investigate suspicious wealth more effectively.

What an overseas entity is

This is a legal entity, such as a company or organisation, that is governed by the law of a country or territory outside the UK.

Read the guidance about overseas entities

What a beneficial owner is

A beneficial owner is any legal body that has significant influence or control over the overseas entity. It can be:

  • an individual person
  • an other legal entity
  • a government or public authority
  • a trustee of a trust
  • a member of a firm that is not a legal person under its governing law

You’ll need to give us details for any registrable beneficial owners that meet one or more of the conditions known as the natures of control.

Who can use this service

You can use this service if you work at, or work on behalf of, the overseas entity. Identity checks must be completed by a third-party agent based in the UK.

What information you need to submit

You'll need to provide information about the overseas entity and any registrable beneficial owners.

Overseas entity information

You'll need to tell us its:

  • name
  • the country it was formed in
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • an email address at the overseas entity we can send important information to, including the Overseas Entity ID
  • its legal form and governing law
  • the public register it’s on and its registration number (if relevant)

You'll also need to tell us if the entity has disposed of UK property or land between 28 February 2022 and [date of launch].

Beneficial owner information: individual person

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • nationality
  • correspondence address and home address
  • the date they became a beneficial owner for the overseas entity
  • nature of control

You'll also need to tell us:

  • if they’re on the UK Sanctions List
  • if any of the entity’s beneficial owners have ever applied to protect personal information at Companies House

Beneficial owner information: other legal entity

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • the public register it’s on and its registration number (if relevant)
  • the date they became a beneficial owner for the overseas entity
  • nature of control

You'll need to tell us if they’re on the UK Sanctions List.

Beneficial owner information: government or public authority

You'll need to tell us each beneficial owner's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • the date they became a beneficial owner for the overseas entity
  • nature of control

If no beneficial owners can be identified

If there are no beneficial owners, you'll need to give us information about the overseas entity's managing officers. This is a director, manager or company secretary of the overseas entity.

Managing officer information: individual

You'll need to tell us each managing officer's:

  • full name (and former names, if relevant)
  • date of birth
  • nationality
  • correspondence address and home address
  • occupation (this is optional)
  • roles and responsibilities in relation to the entity

Managing officer information: corporate

You'll need to tell us each managing officer's:

  • name
  • registered office address and correspondence address
  • legal form and governing law
  • the public register it’s on and its registration number (if relevant)

What identity checks are needed

You must confirm that identity checks have been carried out on all beneficial owners and managing officers. These identity checks must be done by a third-party agent who is based in the UK, such as an accountant or solicitor.

What information will be shown on the public register

Most of the information that is submitted about overseas entities, beneficial owners and managing officers will be publicly available on the Register of Overseas Entities.

We will not show:

  • home addresses
  • full dates of birth – only the month and year will be shown

How to apply to register an overseas entity and its beneficial owners

You'll need:

  • to sign in to or create a Companies House account
  • to give us the name and email address of someone we can contact about the application
  • a credit or debit card to pay the £X fee
Apply to register an overseas entity and its beneficial owners.

What happens after an overseas entity is registered

It can take up to X working days to process an application to register an overseas entity and its beneficial owners. If the application is accepted, the overseas entity and its beneficial owners and managing officers will be added to the Register of Overseas Entities.

If it's rejected, we'll email you to let you know what to do next.

We will also send the Overseas Entity ID number by email. This ID number must be provided to the Land Registry whenever the overseas entity buys, sells or transfers land or property in the UK.

Updating information on the register

The overseas entity must file a statement at least once a year to confirm that the information on the Register of Overseas Entities is up to date.

It may choose to file sooner if it needs to update the information before the next statement is due to be filed.

Removing an overseas entity

An overseas entity can only be removed from the register if it no longer owns any property or land in the UK. Apply to remove an overseas entity from the register by emailing roe@companieshouse.gov.uk.

Privacy statement

What personal information (or ‘data’) we collect

We collect:

  • the name of the obliged entity (if an individual)
  • the email address of the obliged entity (if an individual)
  • the name of the person with significant control (PSC) reported as having a discrepancy
  • the date of birth of the PSC reported as having a discrepancy
  • the nationality of the PSC reported as having a discrepancy
  • the postal address of the PSC reported as having a discrepancy
  • any other personal information voluntarily provided by the person submitting the report

How we use the personal information

The personal information provided by the obliged entity is compared with the relevant information on the public register. If necessary, the information on the public register will be corrected and annotated.

The obliged entity’s personal information will be used to contact them for more information if needed, and to tell them the outcome of the discrepancy report. The information will be stored on the internal Companies House database along with the associated discrepancy report.

The personal information of the obliged entity and the PSC identified in the discrepancy report will not be made available to the public, although the information may already be on the public register for filing purposes.

Where the information will be stored

The information will be held on the Companies House database, but not on the public register.

How long the personal information is stored

Companies House will hold the personal information of the obliged entity and PSC in the discrepancy report on the Companies House database for 10 years.

Legal basis for the use of the personal information

Companies House has a legal obligation to collect this information.

Individual rights

The GDPR provides certain rights that people may carry out regarding their own personal information. If you want to exercise any of these rights, please contact the Data Protection Officer (DPO).

Complaints

If you have a complaint about the way we’re managing your personal information, you can let us know firstly by writing to the DPO at dpo@companieshouse.gov.uk.
